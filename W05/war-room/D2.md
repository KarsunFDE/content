# W5 D2 Tue War-Room — What you're tackling today

> **Mode: Incident.** Morning opens with an Audit Log Search drill you will fail. Afternoon installs OTel + Datadog Agent across all 5 `acquire-gov` components and fixes the failure end-to-end.

## What we're tackling today + why

A Contracting Officer reports that a `POST /draft-solicitation` published at 03:14 last night looks anomalous in the audit binder — and she needs you to reconstruct what happened. You have logs in 5 different services and an AuditEvent row. You will discover (live, in 10 minutes) that the correlation identifiers don't match across surfaces: Angular has a `session_id`, api-gateway has an `X-Request-Id`, solicitation-service has a `correlationId`, ai-orchestrator has a `request_id`, AuditEvent has its own `correlation_id`, and the Bedrock SDK call has nothing. **This is Item 6.** The AIOps blind spot.

This afternoon you install the Datadog Agent into docker-compose, instrument all 5 components with OTel (Datadog RUM on Angular, OTel Java auto-agent on the three Spring services, OTel Python + `gen_ai.*` semconv on ai-orchestrator), add a `traceparent` column to the AuditEvent table, and run the Audit Log Search drill again. By EOD a single W3C `traceparent` joins every surface end-to-end.
