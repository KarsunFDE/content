# W05-SA-2 — Auto-remediation authority (HITL #7)

> **This is HITL touchpoint #7 of 7** — the closing touchpoint in the programme's HITL thread. The ADR produced from this scenario is **load-bearing** for Fri's Final Adversarial PR.

## Client constraint

> *"Our AIOps platform just detected an Item 2 race condition — the publish action on solicitation SOL-2026-0421 succeeded, but the AuditEvent row didn't write. The platform has the original request payload in the OTel trace and could write the missing AuditEvent right now, atomically. The sys_admin is asleep. The CO is offline. The OIG audit binder needs to show every state transition. What's our platform's policy: does the AI write the row, propose it for review, or just page someone?"*
>
> — Karsun engagement lead via the cohort #1 channel, W5 Wed 2026-06-24 03:14

## The programme default

**Propose-and-Await-Approval** is the cohort #1 starting position — but the pair may argue for an alternative with sufficient rationale. The ADR's quality is in the reasoning + the boundary-naming, not in matching the default.

Rationale for the default: FedRAMP AU-9 (audit log integrity) is AC-3-controlled; in federal context, writes to the audit table without human authority can be construed as a separation-of-duties violation (NIST 800-53 AC-5). Propose-and-Await-Approval preserves both AU-2 (audit completeness) and AC-5 (separation of duties).

[NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 AC-5 + AU-9, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/53/r5/upd1/final]
[FedRAMP Rev 5 baselines, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://www.fedramp.gov/rev5/]

## Candidate alternatives the pair researches Wed W5

### Option A — Full Auto

**Position.** Platform writes the missing AuditEvent without human gate. The platform's action is itself an AuditEvent (`replayed_by_ai_sre: true`).

**Argument for.** AU-9 (audit integrity) is *more* protected by writing the row from authoritative OTel trace data than by leaving the gap. The gap is a worse outcome than a programmatic replay. Speed matters when the OIG window is 6 weeks out. The audit-of-the-audit (logging the AI-SRE's action) preserves accountability.

**Argument against.** AC-5 (separation of duties) requires that audit-table writes are AC-3-controlled (access-control enforced). If the AI-SRE has write access, the agent is now a privileged actor on the AU surface — exactly what AC-5 was meant to prevent. Anthropic's responsible-scaling-policy framing suggests AI systems should not have *unsupervised* write authority on safety-critical surfaces.

**Failure mode.** If the OTel trace data was tampered (e.g., a malicious actor with span-write access), the AI-SRE would replay a fabricated AuditEvent under its own authority. The downstream audit binder would show the replayed row as authoritative — a worse outcome than the original gap.

[Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy v1.1, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-s-responsible-scaling-policy]

### Option B — Propose-and-Await-Approval (default)

**Position.** Platform generates the AuditEvent payload from the trace, opens a ticket to sys_admin (or sends a structured incident to PagerDuty), and pages on-call. Human merges the proposed write after review.

**Argument for.** Preserves separation of duties (AC-5) and write-authority controls (AC-3). The proposed payload accelerates the human (the runbook entry has a draft to inspect, not a blank-slate). The audit-of-the-audit cleanly identifies the proposing AI + the approving human.

**Argument against.** If on-call doesn't respond within the OIG window, the gap remains. Adds latency between detection and remediation. Requires the cohort to design the human-approval UX surface (which the cohort hasn't built yet — adds Fri PR scope).

**Failure mode.** Slow on-call response. Mitigation: escalation policy (PagerDuty tier escalation; cohort sys_admin → cohort lead → instructor).

[PagerDuty Incident Response Playbook, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://response.pagerduty.com/]

### Option C — Escalate-only

**Position.** Platform pages on-call human and does *not* generate the payload. Human investigates the trace, decides whether to write, and writes manually.

**Argument for.** Strictest separation of duties; the AI never proposes audit-table content. No risk of the platform proposing a wrong payload that the human rubber-stamps under time pressure. Audit-table writes carry full human attestation.

**Argument against.** Loses the speed advantage. The runbook gets *no* draft from the AI — the human starts from scratch. In a federal context where audit-binder completeness matters, the human's reconstruction may also be incomplete.

**Failure mode.** Human is asleep; gap persists. Same as B but worse — no draft for the human to review when they wake up.

- **Audit-of-the-audit shape** — what AuditEvent does the platform write about itself?
- **Code/config enforcement** — Fri PR includes the code or policy config that enforces the chosen option (not just docs).

## Anti-patterns to call out in defense

- **"It depends."** Not an ADR. Commit to A/B/C with rationale.
- **Cargo-culting Anthropic's RSP framing.** Cite it specifically — which clause supports your choice. Generic "AI shouldn't have authority" arguments are weak.
- **Skipping the audit-of-the-audit.** Whatever option you choose, the platform's action MUST be itself audited. Otherwise you've created an unaudited write path.
- **Treating the OIG audit window as flexible.** The 6-week window is real; an unfilled gap when the binder is requested is a finding against the agency.
- **No code/config enforcement.** An ADR that documents B but the code allows A is rubric-failed on Brownfield Awareness + Code Quality simultaneously.
