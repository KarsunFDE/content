# W05-SA-1 — AIOps platform compare-set

## Client constraint

> *"We're standing up AIOps for our federal acquisitions platform. The agency CIO has heard about four platforms — Datadog, Dynatrace, New Relic, Coralogix — and wants to know why we're picking the one we're picking. Karsun already has Datadog licenses across the org, so 'we already pay for it' is one input. But we need a rigorous defense that doesn't sound like rationalising the incumbent. Walk me through where each platform wins for this system's shape, and tell me where the hands-on choice (Datadog) would be the wrong call."*
>
> — Karsun Delivery Lead via the engagement lead, W5 Mon 2026-06-22

## The programme default

**Datadog AI** (Watchdog AI + LLM Observability) per D-060. Hands-on platform for W5 Tue install onward.

Rationale: cohort-familiar UI; strongest AI-on-AI features in 2026 (Watchdog AI for anomaly detection + LLM Observability for per-request LLM tracing including hallucination signals + cost-per-tenant); Karsun-likely-licensed; FedRAMP High in GovCloud regions; best `/web-research` signal mass for the hot-tech 3-month window.

## Candidate alternatives the pair researches Wed W5

### 1. Dynatrace Davis AI

**Differentiator.** Causation graph approach — Davis builds a topology graph of services + dependencies + changes, then reasons over the graph for root-cause. Other platforms surface *correlations* ("these two metrics moved together"); Davis surfaces *causations* ("this config change caused that latency spike because of this dependency edge").

**Where it would beat Datadog.** Complex multi-service incidents where root-cause requires graph reasoning. `acquire-gov` cohort #1 is 5 components — small enough that Datadog's correlation approach works. Larger systems (Karsun's full CAMEO scope, 20+ services) would benefit more from Dynatrace.

**FedRAMP posture.** FedRAMP Moderate (not High). For workloads requiring High boundary, Dynatrace would need additional controls or wouldn't qualify.

**Cost shape.** Per-host (Dynatrace Platform Subscription / DPS) — predictable but higher entry cost than Datadog's per-host + ingest model for small deployments.

[Dynatrace Davis AI, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://www.dynatrace.com/platform/artificial-intelligence/]

### 2. New Relic AI Monitoring

**Differentiator.** Apdex-driven SRE workflow + per-transaction AI summaries + Errors Inbox AI. NR positions AI as *enhancing existing SRE workflows* rather than replacing them. Strong AI-developer-experience features (the AI helps the developer, not just the on-call).

**Where it would beat Datadog.** Pairs with limited AIOps maturity who want AI as an assistant rather than an autonomous agent. Teams already invested in NR for APM. Per-user pricing aligns better for smaller teams with many seats vs many hosts.

**FedRAMP posture.** FedRAMP Moderate (NR for Federal Government). High-boundary workloads need separate accommodation.

**Cost shape.** Per-user (full-platform user pricing) + ingest. Easier to predict if user count is stable; harder if usage is bursty.

[New Relic AI Monitoring, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://newrelic.com/platform/ai-monitoring]

### 3. Coralogix AI Observability

**Differentiator.** Streaming-pipeline-native — Coralogix's Streama AI processes telemetry as a stream, decoupling storage from query cost. Per-GB ingest model can be dramatically cheaper for log-heavy workloads.

**Where it would beat Datadog.** Log-volume-dominated workloads. A federal-acquisitions platform that's generating high audit-log volume (every state transition, every retrieval, every AI response) can hit Datadog ingest cost cliffs that Coralogix's streaming model avoids.

**FedRAMP posture.** Moderate. Smaller federal-market footprint than Datadog or Dynatrace.

**Cost shape.** Per-GB ingest. Highly predictable; can be cheaper at scale.

[Coralogix AI Observability, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://coralogix.com/ai-observability/]

## Pair's deliverable (consolidated ADR by EOD Wed)

Per `templates/scenario-alternatives.md`:

## Anti-patterns to call out in defense

- **Defaulting to Datadog just because it's the hands-on choice.** The exercise is *honest comparison*, not loyalty to the default. If Dynatrace genuinely wins for `acquire-gov`'s causation-graph case (e.g., cohort grows to 8 services in W6), say so.
- **FedRAMP cargo-cult.** FedRAMP High vs Moderate matters only for workloads crossing the boundary. Not every observability stream needs High.
- **Cost as the only dimension.** Coralogix wins on per-GB cost; that doesn't make it the right pick for cohort #1's shape. Cost is real but isn't the deciding factor for a federal client.
- **Treating "AI-native" as binary.** All four platforms have AI features. The dimension that matters is *what kind of AI* — anomaly detection (Datadog/NR/Coralogix), causation graph (Dynatrace), conversational (Datadog Bits AI, NR AI).

## Probe questions for Live Defense

1. *"You said Datadog's Watchdog AI is anomaly-detection-based. What's the failure mode of anomaly detection on a system with seasonal traffic patterns (e.g., end-of-fiscal-year acquisition spike)?"*
2. *"If we hit Datadog's per-host ingest cap, what's our fallback? Coralogix? OpenSearch Managed (D-050 second light-up)?"*
3. *"Dynatrace's causation graph requires service-topology accuracy. How do we keep that accurate as `acquire-gov` evolves?"*
4. *"The cohort's audit-log volume is going up as W5's instrumentation lands. At what month does Coralogix's per-GB model start beating Datadog's per-host + ingest model? Show the math."*
5. *"FedRAMP High vs Moderate — which `acquire-gov` workloads actually need High? Which can sit on Moderate?"*
