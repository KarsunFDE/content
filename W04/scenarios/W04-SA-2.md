## 4. Comparison dimensions the pair must score

Per `templates/scenario-alternatives.md` §4. **Specific dimensions to weight heavily for this scenario:**

- **Ship-this-week feasibility** — non-negotiable hard constraint. Bedrock Guardrails fails this dimension by D-050 fiat (1-point) regardless of capability.
- **Defense-in-depth posture** — does this layer compose with future layers? Pydantic v2 + Bedrock Guardrails (W5) + LangChain filters (W5+) is the target stacked posture. Whatever the pair picks W4 must compose, not preclude.

## 8. Anti-patterns to call out in defence

- **"We'd just use Bedrock Guardrails"** — without honouring the D-050 deferral. Pair must defend a W4-shippable defense AND name the W5 layered hand-off, not skip the constraint.
- **Input validation only** — without output sanitisation. Per OWASP LLM05:2025, output handling is its own threat class.
- **Regex-only sanitisation** — known fragile; OWASP guidance is to combine syntactic checks with semantic classification (the sandboxed-LLM-evaluation pattern fills this when budget allows).
- **Treating the 4 surfaces as one** — they are 4 different surfaces with different threat profiles (description vs Q&A vs debrief vs CPAR rebuttal). The defense MAY be uniform; the pair must defend that uniformity.
- **Not naming HITL #6 connection** — the LLM06 authority-boundary table (Wed PM Exercise 3) intersects this: which of the 4 surfaces requires CO approval before publish, regardless of whether the input validator passed?
