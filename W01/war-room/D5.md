# W1 Fri — War-Room: Cost + Reliability for LLM Endpoints + Explicit HITL

> **Mode: Incident.** Fri war-room continues Thu's scenario but escalates: the CO from Thu has hit the platform 50 times overnight. Cost report has landed. CO has also surfaced one output she's not comfortable signing off on. Three concerns merge: cost, reliability, and HITL.

## The escalation (10 min framing)

The team lead — instructor — reads from a Slack message:

> *"Team — overnight cost dashboard shows $340 of Bedrock spend on the solicitation drafter. The CO from yesterday hit the endpoint ~50 times trying different drafts. Two questions: (1) is $340 right for what we got back? (2) can we put a cost ceiling on this thing so the program office isn't financially surprised by it. ALSO — the CO flagged that one draft cited '48 CFR 47.305-2' as a paragraph requirement and she can't find that clause anywhere in actual FAR. We need to talk about that."*

The team lead turns to the cohort. *"Three problems. What's our triage order?"*

## Act 1 — Triage discussion (15 min)

Cohort proposes triage. Instructor doesn't override; surfaces trade-offs:

- **Cost-ceiling work is the easiest "ship today" item** — limits + budget alerts.
- **Reliability work** (retry, idempotency, cost-per-request logging) is where the real Friday afternoon time goes.
- **The hallucination ("48 CFR 47.305-2") is the HARDEST problem** — actual answer requires grounding (RAG, which is W2). What can we ship Friday to *manage* the hallucination without yet eliminating it?

The triage order surfaces: **cost ceiling now → cost-per-request logging today → HITL framing for hallucinated outputs → tell CO "grounding ships next week"**.

## Act 2 — Cost as engineering (30 min)

Instructor walks the cohort through cost-as-engineering. Topics:

1. **Per-feature cost tracking** — tag every Bedrock call with feature (`solicitation-draft`, `eval-judge`) + tenant. Aggregate.
2. **Per-tenant budget allocation** — set a daily budget per agency tenant.
3. **Cost ceilings** — what does the platform do when the CO hits the limit? Block? Degrade? Notify?
4. **Caching** — when does it pay back, when does it lie? CO drafting variations on the same prompt — does cache hurt?

Cohort + instructor sketch a per-tenant budget pattern on the whiteboard.

## Act 3 — Reliability primitives (30 min)

The retry/backoff work continues from Thu but with more discipline:

1. **Idempotency** — when is a Bedrock call idempotent? (Read-only inference *should* be, but state-mutating tool calls W3 won't be.)
2. **Retry strategies** — exponential backoff + jitter for 429s + 5xxs; *no* retry for 4xx (other than 429).
3. **When retries are dangerous** — if the cohort retries a call that *did* succeed but the response was lost, they double-spend. Idempotency keys solve this; introduced today, deepened W3 Tue.
4. **Circuit breaker** — when Bedrock is persistently degraded, *fail fast* and surface to CO via HITL escalation rather than retry-storm.

## Act 4 — Explicit HITL framing (30 min)

> **1st of 7 HITL programme touchpoints per D-043+D-044.**

Instructor names HITL explicitly. Frames the question:

**When does an LLM output need a human gate before action?**

Cohort discussion (instructor doesn't pre-answer):

| Reversibility | Blast radius | Federal audit demand | HITL gate? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reversible | Internal only | Low | No gate — log only |
| Reversible | External (CO sees) | Medium | Soft gate — CO confirms before publish |
| Irreversible | External | High | **Hard gate — CO must explicitly approve** |
| Reversible | External | Hallucination-prone | **Soft gate — CO sees flagged outputs** |

The hallucinated "48 CFR 47.305-2" case from Thu is exactly the "reversible-but-hallucination-prone" case — Friday's HITL surface adds a "flagged output requiring CO review" pattern.

Cohort sketches the HITL surface for the `/draft-solicitation` endpoint:
- Confidence threshold below which the output is flagged.
- UX surface (banner / modal / inline) for flagged outputs.
- Audit log entry for every flagged-and-approved or flagged-and-rejected output (federal audit).
- Escalation path when CO is unavailable (queue + notify, not silent ship).

## Anti-patterns to avoid Fri

- **Trying to solve the hallucination problem fully today.** RAG ships next week. Today's work is *managing* the hallucination via HITL, not eliminating it.
- **Cost work as a "Monday job."** Cost was the user-surfaced ask; ignoring it loses cohort credibility with the simulated CO.
- **HITL as a UX afterthought.** The cohort designs the HITL surface *into* the endpoint, not bolted on later.
- **Letting any pair end Fri without an explicit HITL decision committed.** That single ADR is the seed of W2 Thu's RAG fallback + W3's full LangGraph HITL deep-dive.
