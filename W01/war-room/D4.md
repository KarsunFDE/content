# W1 Thu — First FDE War-Room: Solicitation Drafting Under Production Pressure

> **Mode: Incident.** First real war-room of the cohort. Instructor frames a realistic federal-acquisitions scenario; cohort works it. Phase 1 of the Pair Project starts today.

## The scenario (10 min framing)

**You're on engagement at the General Services Administration's Federal Acquisition Service.** A contracting officer (CO) has emailed your team lead:

> *"We need to draft a solicitation for cloud modernization services for a sub-agency by Friday. Last month's solicitation for similar work took the CO three weeks of back-and-forth with the program office; we're hoping the AI drafter your team is piloting can compress that to two days. The CO is willing to be a guinea pig but she also doesn't want her name on something that hallucinates a FAR clause. What can the platform actually do this week?"*

The team lead — instructor — turns to the cohort. *"What's the honest answer? What does the platform need to do, and what does it definitely not yet do?"*

## Act 1 — Comprehension (40 min)

Cohort explores the training-project's `/draft-solicitation` endpoint as-is:
- The current `ai-orchestrator/app/main.py` (the deliberately-naive Bedrock invocation).
- The Spring solicitation-service call path.
- The Angular UX (spinner-then-wall-of-text).

**Cohort prompts (instructor seeds when needed):**

1. *"Run the current endpoint against a real test prompt. What happens when Bedrock rate-limits us?"* → 429 propagates as a 500 to Angular. No retry.
2. *"What does the response look like when Bedrock returns truncated JSON?"* → Spring side gets `KeyError`.
3. *"Can the contracting officer trust this output?"* → No grounding, no citations, no faithfulness check.
4. *"How would we tell the CO that the platform tried but couldn't produce a draft she'd want?"* → No error UX; no HITL escalation path.

**Output by 60 min:** cohort writes a one-page **gap analysis** in `weeks/W01/thu-gap-analysis.md` — *what the platform does, what it doesn't, and what we can ship by Friday EOD.*

## Act 2 — Ship plan (30 min)

Cohort + instructor build a ship plan for Thu–Fri:

| Block | Deliverable |
|-------|-------------|
| Thu afternoon | Production-quality Bedrock invocation: retry + backoff, cost tracking, streaming, system-prompt architecture |
| Fri morning war-room | Cost + reliability framing |
| Fri afternoon | Structured output (Pydantic) + output validation gates + context engineering + dynamic context assembly + context compression + **explicit HITL** + async FastAPI integration |
| EOD Fri | First PR Adversarial Review (Light) on each pair's Phase 1 plan-spec |

Each pair commits a slice of the ship plan into their `specs/week-1.md`. Same `/draft-solicitation` endpoint owned by all three pairs; pairs work parallel slices on the same training-project repo.

## Act 3 — Live modelling (40 min)

Instructor models how an FDE *actually* approaches the work — narrated:

1. *"I'm going to ask Claude to read the current endpoint + propose the smallest change that adds retry + backoff without breaking anything else."*
2. Walks through Claude's proposal, shows where it's right, where it's missing context.
3. *"Now I'm going to ask Claude for the failing test that proves the retry works."* — TDD-style discipline introduced here.
4. *"Watch what happens when I just paste Claude's output without reading it."* — runs it; finds the bug Claude introduced (e.g., wrong `botocore` exception name); narrates the *recovery*.

Cohort observes that Claude is an accelerant, not an oracle. Reading the diff is mandatory.

## Act 4 — Practical block kickoff (60 min)

Cohort splits into pairs. Each pair takes a slice of the ship plan and starts implementing.

Instructor circulates. War-room mode ends; instructor is now on-call rather than driving.

**Critical FDE pattern surfaced today:** working from a *plan that survives contact with reality*. The plan written Mon won't survive Tuesday verbatim — that's expected. The discipline is *naming what changed and why* in the plan-spec retro pattern (which formally arrives W3 Mon per D-036; W2 Mon previews it).

## Output artifacts (EOD Thu)

- `weeks/W01/thu-gap-analysis.md` — one-page gap analysis the cohort co-authored.
- First Pair Project Phase 1 commits (each pair contributes to a shared `/draft-solicitation` improvement OR to their own pair Project's first endpoint — instructor's call based on cohort pace).
- First Codex Adversarial Reviews on Thu PRs (Light strictness per D-034).

## Anti-patterns to avoid Thu

- **Cohort waiting for the instructor to "tell them what to do."** Today is the first day the cohort owns the work. Instructor circulates, doesn't drive.
- **Pair-Project domain alignment with the training-project.** The Pair Project domain should be the pair's choice — different from federal acquisitions so the cohort experiences AI adoption in a fresh context.
- **Skipping the gap analysis.** The discipline of *naming what the platform doesn't yet do* is half the FDE skill.
- **Letting Codex findings pile up.** Light strictness or not, P0 findings must close before EOD.
