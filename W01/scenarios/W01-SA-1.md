# W01-SA-1 — Bedrock vs OpenAI direct

## Client constraint

> *"We're piloting AI for the General Services Administration's Federal Acquisition Service. Procurement leadership has surfaced a question: we know Karsun's training stack is on AWS Bedrock, but our agency CIO got a separate pitch from a vendor that OpenAI direct API would be faster and cheaper. The CIO doesn't actually know what FedRAMP is and is asking your team to either confirm Bedrock or pivot to OpenAI. We have one week to decide before the program office commits the licensing budget. What do we tell her?"*
>
> — Agency CIO via the Karsun engagement lead, W1 Fri 2026-XX-XX

## The programme default

**AWS Bedrock with Claude** (per ADR-0002 in the training-project repo). Bedrock is FedRAMP High in GovCloud regions; cross-region inference gives higher throughput within the boundary.

## Candidate alternatives the pair researches Wed W2

1. **OpenAI direct API** — fastest market access; broadest model catalog; but **FedRAMP gap** as of Q1 2026.
2. **Azure OpenAI** — has FedRAMP High via Azure Government; different commercial relationship; different SLA shape.

## Pair's deliverable (ADR + Live Defense)

The pair authors an ADR by Thu W2 EOD covering:

- **Decision** — which platform does the pair recommend?
- **Context** — federal data-residency posture, FedRAMP requirements, cost shape per-token, model capability differences.
- **Alternatives** — both alternatives evaluated, not just dismissed.
- **Consequences** — what happens if the pair's recommendation lands wrong?
- **Rollback story** — if the agency CIO comes back in 3 months and wants to pivot, what's the migration cost?

## Anti-patterns to call out in defence

- **Defaulting to Bedrock just because the training stack uses it.** The exercise is *honest comparison*, not loyalty to the default. If the pair concludes OpenAI direct genuinely wins for this client's profile, they should say so.
- **FedRAMP misuse** — the pair must understand FedRAMP Moderate vs High vs P-ATO. The Agency CIO doesn't know FedRAMP; the pair must explain it at three levels (executive, summary, technical).
- **Cost as the only dimension** — the cohort defending on cost alone reveals shallow analysis. Cost is real but is rarely the deciding factor for a federal client.
- **Treating "FedRAMP Authorized" as binary** — it's a *boundary*; some workloads cross it, some don't. Pair must reason about *which* workloads need the boundary.

## Probe questions for Live Defense

The defender should be ready for:

1. *"You said Bedrock is FedRAMP High. Is that true for every Bedrock model or just some?"*
2. *"If we hit Azure OpenAI's quota, what's our fallback? Bedrock?"*
3. *"What does the Agency CIO actually need to know — and what's noise?"*
4. *"What's the cost shape difference at our expected request volume?"*
5. *"If the CIO insists on OpenAI direct despite the FedRAMP gap, what do we recommend she do to mitigate?"*
