# W06-SA-2 — HITL authority boundary matrix — full-auto vs propose-and-await vs escalate-only per touchpoint

## Client constraint

> *"Your handoff package includes a HITL authority-boundaries document. That doc has to answer one question for any reader (Karsun manager, OIG auditor, next FDE): for each AI endpoint in your system, what's the AI allowed to do alone, what does it need human approval for, and what does it escalate vs decide-and-log?"*
>
> *"Two complications: (1) you have 7 HITL touchpoints from across the programme (per D-043/D-044), each in a different shape. (2) you have 8 AI endpoints in ai-orchestrator + your pair-project's own AI surfaces. How do you organize the doc?"*
>

## Authority levels (the columns of the matrix)

- **full-auto** — AI executes without human approval; result logged; reversibility + low blast radius assumed.
- **propose-and-await-approval** — AI proposes; human approves before persistence. Audit log captures both proposal + approval.
- **escalate-only** — AI flags + suspends action; routes to human queue. Examples: cost-ceiling escalation, anomaly detection on input.
- **never-AI** — explicitly forbidden by regulation or pair-decision. Example: SSA award decision (FAR 15.308 SSA cannot delegate) — `/eval/ssa-review` AI assists drafting only, never decides.

## The shape choice

### Shape A — Per-endpoint × per-touchpoint matrix (default)

A table with 8+N rows (endpoints) × 7 columns (touchpoints). Each cell either lists the authority level or `N/A` if the touchpoint doesn't apply to that endpoint.

Pros: explicit, scannable, exhaustive; the OIG-Auditor's preferred shape.
Cons: large matrix (56+ cells); reader has to triangulate "what does this endpoint actually DO?" from the matrix.

### Shape B — Per-touchpoint narrative (7 sections, each with affected endpoints listed)

7 numbered sections (one per touchpoint), each names which endpoints exhibit the touchpoint + at what authority level.

Pros: narrative flow; reads as a story of "here's how HITL shows up across the system"; easier for Karsun-manager skim.
Cons: hides per-endpoint clarity (a reader asking "what can `/draft-amendment` do alone?" has to scan 7 sections).

### Shape C — Per-blast-radius classification (3 buckets, endpoints sorted)

Three sections: `low-blast-radius (full-auto eligible)` / `medium (propose-and-await)` / `high (escalate-only or never-AI)`. Each endpoint lands in exactly one bucket; the per-touchpoint nuance lives in a per-endpoint sub-bullet.

Pros: blast-radius framing matches how Karsun managers actually reason about staffing AI risk; reduces cognitive load (3 buckets vs 7 touchpoints).
Cons: collapses the cohort's programme-wide HITL thread (the 7 touchpoints) — D-043's "make HITL visible at 7 touchpoints" design intent is partially lost.

## What the pair commits Wed PM

The pair picks ONE shape and authors `docs/hitl-authority-boundaries.md` instantiated from `weeks/W06/hitl-authority-boundaries.md` (canonical template, Shape A scaffolded). If picking Shape B or C, the pair restructures the canonical template.

## Anti-patterns to call out

- **Defaulting to Shape A without populating cells** — empty cells = "we haven't decided"; populated cells = the synthesis. Empty cells fail the OIG-Auditor depth check.
- **Picking Shape B for ease of authoring** when the audit-trail demand is real — Shape B is harder to audit per-endpoint. Defendable only if the matrix-style table appears as an appendix.
- **Shape C without naming the D-043 7-touchpoint visibility loss** — D-043's design intent is HITL repetition as muscle-building. Shape C trades that for blast-radius clarity. Pair should name this tradeoff in the doc.
- **Forgetting the pair-project AI endpoints** — the doc must cover both acquire-gov endpoints AND the pair-project's AI surfaces. Pairs that ship a doc covering only acquire-gov endpoints have a coverage gap.
- **`/eval/ssa-review` listed as anything other than `never-AI` for the decision step** — FAR 15.308 SSA cannot delegate. AI can DRAFT the SSDD; AI cannot DECIDE the award. Misclassification here is a regulatory red flag.

## Recommended shape per pair profile

| Pair profile | Recommended shape |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Pair-project has >2 AI endpoints + thick agentic flows | Shape A (full matrix — the audit-trail demand is real) |
| Pair-project has 1 narrow AI surface + Karsun-manager audience prioritized | Shape B (narrative — fits manager-tier skim) |
| Pair under time pressure Wed + reasoning ergonomics matter more than completeness | Shape C (blast-radius — fastest to populate, defendable trade-off) |
