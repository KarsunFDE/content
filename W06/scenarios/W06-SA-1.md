# W06-SA-1 — Handoff package depth: minimal vs comprehensive

## Client constraint

> *"Your pair-project is being handed off to a Karsun manager Thursday afternoon. They have 30 minutes to skim the deliverable before they staff a new FDE onto it next quarter. Same package goes to an OIG-style auditor who'll spend a full day reading it. And the next FDE picking it up will live in it for 6 months. How thick is the right deliverable?"*
>
> — Cohort-internal framing, W6 Tue 13:00

## The shape choice

Three candidate handoff-package shapes the pair can ship:

### Shape A — Minimal (3 artifacts)

- `docs/handoff.md` — one comprehensive README (~10 pages)
- `docs/runbook.md` — operational runbook
- `eval/REPORT.md` — eval report

Pros: low cognitive load on Karsun manager (one place to start); cheap to maintain in Phase 3.
Cons: hides decision provenance (no ADRs); attestation language scattered; HITL authority not surfaced cleanly.
**Karsun-manager response:** "Easy to skim. But where's the audit trail?"
**OIG-Auditor response:** "Where's the artifact for the W4 Fri incident closure? Where's the FedRAMP control mapping?" — Shape A FAILS this depth check.

### Shape B — Comprehensive (8 artifacts) — default

- `docs/runbook.md` + `docs/adrs/INDEX.md` + `eval/REPORT.md` + `docs/known-weaknesses.md` + `docs/handoff.md` + `docs/security-attestation.md` + `docs/hitl-authority-boundaries.md` + `docs/threat-model.md` + `docs/cost-analysis.md`

Pros: complete audit-trail; passes both Karsun-manager skim + OIG-Auditor depth; HITL synthesis stands as separate capstone artifact (per W6 design).
Cons: maintenance cost; risk of artifact-rot in Phase 3; new FDE might not know which one to read first.
**Karsun-manager response:** "Comprehensive. Tell me which page I read first, though."
**OIG-Auditor response:** "Excellent. Walk me through the `Finding` model."

### Shape C — Layered/tiered (5 artifacts with tiered depth)

- Tier-1 (5-min read): `docs/EXECUTIVE-SUMMARY.md` — Karsun-manager facing
- Tier-2 (30-min read): `docs/handoff.md` — new-FDE facing
- Tier-3 (depth-on-demand): `docs/runbook.md` + `docs/adrs/INDEX.md` + `docs/security-attestation.md` (with HITL boundaries folded into security-attestation.md)

Pros: explicit reader-aware tiering; Karsun manager + new FDE + OIG auditor each have a "start here" doc; fewer artifacts to maintain.
Cons: folding HITL boundaries INTO security-attestation hides the synthesis (it's the W6 capstone — should it have its own home?); requires writing 3 audiences worth of intro material, which doubles authoring cost.
**Karsun-manager response:** "I like the tiering. But did you give up the HITL synthesis as a standalone?"
**OIG-Auditor response:** "Tier-3 depth is good. Show me where the eval report lives."

## What the pair commits Tue PM

The pair picks ONE shape and authors handoff Tue-Wed against it. The choice IS the artifact — no formal ADR, but the rationale lands in `docs/handoff.md` §Shape rationale.

## Anti-patterns to call out

- **Defaulting to Shape B because "comprehensive sounds safer."** Shape B has real maintenance cost. If your pair can't commit to Phase 3 maintenance of 8 artifacts, Shape C or A may be the honest choice.
- **Shape A without naming the audit-trail gap.** Shape A is defensible IF the pair names the OIG-depth gap as a Phase-3 known-weakness. Shape A claimed as production-ready is dishonest.
- **Shape C without writing the tier-1 executive summary first.** The tier-1 doc is the hardest to write because it requires distillation. Pairs who pick C and leave tier-1 for last will ship a weak executive summary.
- **Letting the W6 capstone HITL synthesis disappear into another doc.** The synthesis IS the W6 deliverable per the brief. Shapes A and C either subsume or omit it — defendable only if explicitly named in the handoff README.

## Probe questions for Wed FDE SME Q&A

The defender should be ready for:

1. *"Which shape did you pick? Why?"*
2. *"Karsun manager's reading the deliverable in 30 minutes — what's the page they read first?"*
3. *"OIG-Auditor's reading it in 8 hours. Show me the artifact that takes the longest to audit. Why?"*
4. *"What's your Phase-3 maintenance commitment? Who keeps these artifacts current?"*
5. *"If you had to drop ONE of these artifacts to ship Thursday, which? Why?"*

## Recommended shape per pair profile

| Pair profile | Recommended shape |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Phase 2 work surfaced 4+ known-weaknesses still open | Shape B (comprehensive — surface every weakness as own `Finding` in `known-weaknesses.md`) |
| Pair-project is narrow vertical (FOIA single workflow) with cleaner state | Shape C (layered — fewer artifacts to maintain) |
| Pair under time pressure Tue-Wed (codex P0 findings stacking up) | Shape A + honest gap-naming (don't pretend depth that isn't there) |
