## 3. FedRAMP attestation language (10 min)

Your security attestation (`docs/security-attestation.md`) maps your controls to **FedRAMP Moderate Rev 5** + **OWASP LLM Top 10 (2025 v2.0)**.

[FedRAMP Rev 5 Authorization Baselines, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://www.fedramp.gov/rev5/baselines/] ‚Äî Moderate is the right tier for the kind of work acquire-gov represents (federal data, not classified). Rev 5 is the current revision as of 2026-05-23.

[OWASP LLM Top 10 2025 v2.0, retrieved 2026-05-23, https://genai.owasp.org/llm-top-10/] ‚Äî note the 2025 list reordered + reframed some categories from the 2023 list; use the 2025 v2.0 list, not earlier.

Three buckets for attestation language:

- **Controls met** ‚Äî "Implemented and verified. See `<artifact-path>` for evidence."
- **Controls met with gaps** ‚Äî "Implemented with named gap. Gap: `<one-sentence>`. Tracked as `Finding/F-2026-W6-NNN`."
- **Controls not met (blocker for next FAR-clause-eligible deployment)** ‚Äî "Not implemented. Blocker for FedRAMP authorization at Moderate. Remediation owner: `<role>`. ETA: `<date>`."

**Honesty rule:** never claim "controls met" without a linked artifact. Karsun-manager and OIG-Auditor both probe this ‚Äî they'd rather see 5 honest "controls met with gap" entries than 50 "controls met" claims with no evidence. The W6 Wed dry-run will surface this if you got it wrong.

For each acquire-gov debt item (1-12) you actually closed in W4-W5: include it as "controls met" with the PR link as evidence. For items still open (Item 12 GHA lint is the canonical example ‚Äî it's intentionally left as Phase 3 work): include it as "controls met with gap" with the Phase 3 `Finding` ID.
