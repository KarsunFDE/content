# W03-SA-1 — Single-agent vs multi-agent for intake-triage

## 1. The client constraint

> "We have an architecture decision and I want your team to defend the call. You built the intake-triage as a single-agent ReAct loop Mon-Tue and it works. But Wed-Fri you're building the evaluator → consensus → SSA flow as multi-agent. Operationally, do I want my intake-triage to *match* the evaluator-flow shape (multi-agent for consistency) or *differ* from it (single-agent because it's a simpler problem)? I have CS staff who'll need to debug this thing at 23:00 when a vendor calls to ask why their proposal got escalated. Which shape is the right one — and what's the cost of being wrong?"
>
> — Contracting Officer, W3 Wed afternoon, captured during the scenario-research slot

## 2. The default programme answer

**Single-agent ReAct loop** (as shipped Mon-Tue). The intake-triage is fundamentally a *one-decision* problem: given a proposal, what's the disposition (route to evaluators, escalate to CO, request vendor amendment ack)? ReAct's read-reason-act loop maps cleanly to the decision shape. Operationally simpler — one LangGraph thread per proposal, one tool-call sequence, one trace per triage event. CS staff can read one trace top-to-bottom.

## 3. Candidate alternatives the pair evaluates

| Alternative | One-line capability | Maturity |
|-------------|---------------------|----------|
| Supervisor-worker multi-agent | Architectural consistency with Wed-Fri evaluator-flow; supervisor delegates to specialist workers (acknowledgement-checker, evaluator-router, escalation-handler) | LangGraph 0.6+ stable; pattern widely-deployed (verified 2026-05-23 via /web-research) |
| Hand-rolled coordinator (no agent framework) | Plain Python function with conditional logic; calls Bedrock directly when reasoning is needed | Always-available; no framework dependency; debugging is print-statements |

## 4. Comparison dimensions the pair scores (1–5)

For each alternative (and the default single-agent), score on:

- **Capability fit** — does it solve the triage problem without compromise?
- **Cost shape** — Bedrock invocations per triage event (single-agent: 1-3; multi-agent: 5-12; hand-rolled: 1-2 with explicit branches). At expected volume (50/day during TEP weeks, 5/day off-peak), what's the monthly Bedrock cost?
- **Operational burden** — what does the 23:00 CS-staff debug session look like? How many LangSmith tabs? How many tool-call traces?
- **Security posture** — multi-tenant boundary (Item 10) implementation surface. Where can the wrong-agency-id leak?
- **Maturity** — LangGraph multi-agent: stable; hand-rolled: timeless but you wrote it; single-agent: stable.
- **Exit cost** — if 6 months from now you want to switch shapes, how much work? (Single-agent → multi-agent is *easier* than multi-agent → single-agent because you're decomposing not collapsing.)

## 5. Required pair deliverable (in their pair-project repo)

A short ADR (≤2 pages) at `adrs/W03-SA-1.md`:

- **Decision:** which shape does the pair recommend for *production*?
- **Context:** 1-paragraph re-statement of the CO constraint (consistency vs simplicity).
- **Considered:** all three alternatives evaluated, scored 1-5 across the six dimensions above.
- **Consequences:** what does the pair give up by choosing it? What's the rollback story (single → multi or multi → single migration)?
- **References:** dated citations for every quantitative claim (Bedrock cost numbers, LangGraph stability claims, etc.) via /web-research.

## 8. Anti-patterns to call out in defense

- **"Multi-agent is more sophisticated therefore better."** Sophistication is not a value. The CS staff debug at 23:00 is the value.
- **"We chose multi-agent for consistency with the evaluator-flow."** Consistency isn't free — it's paid in operational complexity. Defend the *trade*, not the consistency claim.
- **Cost-per-triage estimates without a citation.** Bedrock pricing is documented; verify your numbers via /web-research before defending them.
- **Treating Item 10 as the same risk surface in both shapes.** Multi-agent has *more places to forget the agency_id filter*. Single-agent has one. The defender should know the count.
- **Forgetting the exit-cost asymmetry.** Single-agent → multi-agent is *decomposition* (cheap). Multi-agent → single-agent is *consolidation* (expensive). Choosing multi-agent locks more.
