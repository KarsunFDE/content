## 1. Regulatory framework

| Regulation | Scope | Owner | Karsun framing |
|------------|-------|-------|----------------|
| **Inspector General Act of 1978** (5 USC App. 3 → recodified at 5 USC §§ 401–424 after 2022 reorg) | Establishes statutory OIGs in federal agencies; independence; reporting; semiannual reports | Congress | The constitutional basis for OIGs. Defines independence + dual reporting (head of agency + Congress). |
| **IG Empowerment Act of 2016 (Pub. L. 114-317)** | Expanded subpoena + records-access authority; testimonial subpoenas; CIGIE legal counsel | Congress | Closed agency-loophole that blocked OIG access |
| **IG Act amendments — NDAA 2022 / James M. Inhofe NDAA (Pub. L. 117-263 §§ 5271–5274)** | Removal limitations; whistleblower retaliation protections; vacancy reporting | Congress | Independence reinforcement |
| **CIGIE Quality Standards (Yellow Book / GAGAS)** | Generally Accepted Government Auditing Standards | GAO | Audit-quality framework all OIG audits follow |
| **CIGIE Quality Standards for Investigations** | Investigation framework | CIGIE | Investigation-quality framework |
| **OMB Circular A-50** | Audit followup — agency response to audit findings | OMB | The audit-response rulebook on the agency side |
| **OMB Circular A-123** | Internal controls + ERM | OMB | Related — internal-control framework that OIG audits against |
| **31 USC § 3801 (Program Fraud Civil Remedies Act)** | Authority for OIG-driven civil penalties | Congress | Penalty mechanism short of criminal |
| **Whistleblower Protection Act** | Whistleblower protection (5 USC § 2302) | OSC | Common source of OIG complaints |

**Authority hierarchy:** Statute (IG Act + amendments) → CIGIE standards → OMB followup guidance (A-50) → agency-specific OIG charter + audit protocols.

## 2. OIG ecosystem — who's who

| Entity | What it is |
|--------|-----------|
| **OIG** (Office of Inspector General) | Statutory office in 70+ federal entities |
| **Inspector General (IG)** | Head — appointed by President (Senate-confirmed for large agencies; designated federal-entity-head for smaller) |
| **Deputy IG / AIGs** | Senior leadership: Audit, Investigations, Evaluations, Counsel |
| **CIGIE** (Council of the Inspectors General on Integrity and Efficiency) | Government-wide OIG coordinating body |
| **Office of Special Counsel (OSC)** | Separate; handles federal whistleblower retaliation + prohibited personnel practices |
| **GAO** (Government Accountability Office) | Legislative-branch counterpart; broader government-wide oversight |
| **Pandemic Response Accountability Committee (PRAC)** | Cross-OIG body — pandemic-funds oversight |
| **DOJ Public Integrity Section** | Receives OIG criminal referrals |
| **OMB** | Audit-followup policy ownership |

## 3. OIG work-product types

| Product | What it is | Cadence |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Audit report** | Findings + recommendations on programs/operations | Variable; major audits = months to a year |
| **Inspection / Evaluation report** | Less-formal-than-audit assessment | Variable |
| **Investigation report** | Allegation of fraud/waste/abuse | Variable; sometimes years |
| **Management advisory / alert memo** | Time-sensitive issue surfacing | Days to weeks |
| **Semiannual Report to Congress** | Statutory — every 6 months | Apr 30 / Oct 31 |
| **Top Management Challenges** | Annual — identifies agency's biggest risks | Annual |
| **Peer Review report** | CIGIE-coordinated peer review of OIG itself | Triennial typically |

## 4. Workflow stages — OIG audit + agency response lifecycle

| Stage | When | Reg anchor | What happens |
|-------|------|-----------|--------------|
| 1. **Audit planning** | OIG-internal | CIGIE Quality Standards | OIG identifies audit topic from risk assessment + congressional request + hotline + Top Mgmt Challenges |
| 2. **Audit announcement** | Project start | CIGIE standards | OIG notifies agency of audit objectives + scope + entry conference |
| 3. **Fieldwork** | Project execution | CIGIE standards | OIG collects evidence; interviews; document requests; site visits |
| 4. **Discussion drafts** | Pre-issuance | CIGIE standards | OIG shares preliminary findings with agency to verify factual accuracy |
| 5. **Draft report + agency comment cycle** | Pre-issuance | A-50 + CIGIE | Formal draft to agency; agency formal response (typically 30 days); agency may concur / non-concur / partially concur per recommendation |
| 6. **Final report issuance** | Project close | IG Act | Posted to oversight.gov (most public; some restricted) + transmitted to agency + Congress |
| 7. **Agency Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** | Post-issuance | A-50 + agency reg | Agency develops CAPs for each recommendation |
| 8. **Followup + closure** | Until each recommendation closed | A-50 | OIG monitors recommendation closure; some remain open for years |
| 9. **Semiannual reporting** | Statutory cycle | IG Act § 405 | OIG reports open recs + dollar values to Congress |
| 10. **Top Mgmt Challenges report** | Annual | IG Act | Forward-looking risk identification |

**Investigation lifecycle (separate from audit):**

| Stage | What happens |
|-------|--------------|
| 1. **Hotline / complaint intake** | OIG receives allegation (whistleblower, public hotline, congressional, internal) |
| 2. **Triage** | Determine jurisdiction + open investigation or refer |
| 3. **Investigation** | Subpoena authority; coordinate with DOJ on criminal matters |
| 4. **Resolution** | Criminal referral / civil penalty / administrative action / unsubstantiated closure |
| 5. **Reporting** | If founded, may include in semiannual report (with redactions) |

## 5. Key terminology

| Term | What it means |
|------|---------------|
| **Yellow Book / GAGAS** | Generally Accepted Government Auditing Standards (GAO-issued) — the federal audit standard |
| **Red Book** | GAO's Principles of Federal Appropriations Law — companion reference |
| **CAP** (Corrective Action Plan) | Agency's response to each audit recommendation |
| **Open recommendation** | Recommendation not yet implemented by agency |
| **Disallowed cost** | Questioned + ultimately rejected as unallowable |
| **Funds put to better use** | Identified savings the agency could realize |
| **Concur / Non-concur / Partial concur** | Agency's formal stance per recommendation |
| **Followup** | A-50-mandated tracking of CAP execution |
| **Subpoena duces tecum** | Document subpoena (OIG has under §404 of IG Act) |
| **Testimonial subpoena** | Sworn-testimony subpoena (added 2016 IG Empowerment Act, narrow conditions) |
| **MOU / referral** | When OIG refers matter to another OIG or DOJ |
| **Hotline** | Public-facing complaint channel each OIG operates |
| **Whistleblower** | Federal employee / contractor employee reporting waste, fraud, abuse — statutorily protected from retaliation |
| **Independence — institutional + organizational + personal** | Three independence dimensions GAGAS requires |
| **Material weakness / significant deficiency** | Internal-control finding tiers |
| **Questioned cost** | Cost identified during audit as potentially unallowable |
| **Final audit determination** | Cognizant agency's official disposition of questioned costs |
| **CIGIE Award / Recognition** | OIG community professional recognition |

## 7. Where AI adoption + brownfield modernization typically lands

### Phase-1 seeds

| Seed | Brownfield pain | AI-adoption surface |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Audit-evidence pre-staging** | Agency staff manually pull docs in response to OIG requests | Auto-stage from document store keyed to audit objectives |
| **CAP draft assist** | Agency drafts CAPs from scratch per recommendation | Generate CAP from recommendation + similar past CAPs |
| **Open-recommendation tracking** | Agencies lose track of multi-year open recs | Auto-trace recommendation → CAP → milestone → closure evidence |
| **Hotline triage** | OIG hotline receives high volume; manual triage | Allegation-classification + duplicate detection |
| **Cross-OIG correlation** | Similar issues across agencies missed | Topic modeling across oversight.gov public reports |
| **Audit-report drafting** | OIG audit reports written from scratch | Draft narrative + finding-language from evidence + GAGAS templates |
| **Top-mgmt-challenges synthesis** | Annual report syntheses agency-wide risk | Roll up from year's audit + investigation work |
| **Semiannual report assembly** | Statutory cadence + heavy lift every 6 months | Auto-pull stats + report drafts |

### Phase-2 seeds

| Seed | What modernizes |
|------|-----------------|
| **Continuous auditing platforms** | Always-on indicator monitoring vs periodic audits |
| **Recommendation-CAP-closure data model** | First-class lifecycle, not spreadsheet |
| **Whistleblower-intake modernization** | Secure + tracked + ombudsman-aware |
| **Cross-agency audit knowledge graph** | Findings reusable across similar program structures |
| **Audit-response-readiness scoring** | Pre-OIG-arrival readiness check |

### Patterns to flag

- **OIG independence is constitutional-grade.** AI tools the agency provides to its OIG cannot be designed in ways that compromise independence.
- **Subpoena handling needs human-in-the-loop.** AI cannot interpret subpoena scope.
- **Whistleblower confidentiality is statutory.** AI ingestion of investigation records must preserve identity protection.
- **Records-retention for OIG records varies.** Audit working papers retain 7 years (GAGAS); criminal-investigation records longer.
- **No AI in criminal cases without DOJ coordination.** OIG criminal investigations route through DOJ — AI-generated evidence-summaries need disclosure-discipline.
- **Defamation + Privacy-Act risk on individual findings.** Investigations naming individuals carry both.
